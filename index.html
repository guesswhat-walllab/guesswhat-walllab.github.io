About

This game is a research study out of Stanford University for parents of children between the ages of 3 and 12 years. Families who participate are helping researchers in the Wall Lab use machine learning and artificial intelligence to analyze behaviors expressed by children while interacting with family members via home video.

Choose between 25+ different decks and play this exciting charades game on your phone with your kids, and optionally, help contribute to autism research by sharing your videos with the research team.

Q:  How do we play the Quiz Challenge decks?

A:  Once a Quiz Challenge deck is selected:

  • Parents: Sit with your child on your lap or next to you. The phone will play a short 15 second prompt with a question at the bottom. Read your child the question and prompt them to answer correctly. If they are correct, tilt the phone downward to award a point. If they are incorrect, tilt the phone upward to skip!
  • Kids: Answer the question your parent or guardian is asking to the best of your knowledge!

Q: How do we play the Charades Challenge decks?

A: Once a Charades Challenge deck is selected:

  • Parents: Place the phone on your forehead facing your child. When the game starts, you (the parent) need to guess what the child is acting-out.
  • Kids: Act out what you see on the screen! For example, if you see a happy face, smile as best as you can!
